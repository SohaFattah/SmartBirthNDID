SmartBirth NDID – Newborn Digital Identity System

A national concept that creates a secure digital identity for every newborn at the moment of birth using biometric fingerprint capture, smart hospital wristbands, and direct integration with the Absher platform.

Problem

Newborn registration in many hospitals is still manual and paper-based. This leads to delays, duplicated effort, and potential human errors that may impact both families and hospital staff.

  • Multiple manual steps to issue a birth certificate and update the family registry.
  • Risk of misidentification or newborn switching inside crowded nurseries.
  • Delayed access to e-government services that depend on the child’s digital identity.

Solution – SmartBirth NDID

SmartBirth NDID introduces a secure, automated workflow that links the newborn’s biometric identity to national platforms from the first minutes of life.

  • Capture infant-safe fingerprint data directly after birth.
  • Create a unique digital ID and link it to the family registry in Absher.
  • Print and assign a smart wristband connected to the newborn’s digital identity.
  • Record birth events and movements in the medical record using AI-enabled cameras.

End-to-End Workflow

The prototype simulates the full journey of the newborn inside the hospital and across connected systems:

Birth event recorded Fingerprint captured Digital ID generated Data sent to Absher Smart wristband printed Medical record updated

Testing / Validation

We validated the concept by walking through this complete sequence and checking that each step can be mapped to existing hospital and government systems. The focus is on data consistency, secure integration, and preventing any mismatch between the physical newborn and the digital identity.

Key Technologies

  • Infant-safe biometric fingerprint scanner integrated with hospital systems.
  • Secure API layer for communication with the Absher platform.
  • Smart newborn wristbands with QR / ID encoding for real-time verification.
  • AI-assisted cameras to track newborn movements and link events to the record.
  • Integration with EMR / EHR systems for automatic documentation of birth events.
